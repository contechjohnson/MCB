# Contact Objections Analysis Report

## Executive Summary

Analyzed 5,733 contacts with documented objections (excluded 11,402 rows where objections = "NONE").

| Category | Count | % of Total | Notes |

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Price is the #1 Barrier (39.3%)

**Common phrases:**
- "Can't afford"
- "Too expensive"
- "Budget concern"
- "Beyond current budget"
- "Not ready to make financial commitments"
- "Working two jobs"

**Example quotes:**
- "Concerned about cost due to working two jobs"
- "Pricing constraint due to being a mom of four"
- "Price feels too high, Doesn't have the money, Can't afford the program"
- "Too expensive without insurance, budget concern"

**Insight:** Nearly 40% of objections relate to affordability. Payment plans and financing options (Denefits) are critical.

---

### 2. Insurance Coverage Questions (17.0%)

**Common phrases:**
- "Insurance coverage"
- "How to submit to insurance"
- "Does insurance cover this?"
- "HSA eligible?"

**Example quotes:**
- "Asked about insurance coverage and how to submit to insurance"
- "Asked about HSA, wants to take a day or two to decide based on price"
- "Asked about insurance, payment structure, deductible"

**Insight:** Clear insurance/HSA documentation could reduce friction for 17% of leads.

---

### 3. "Will Book" (13.7%) - Positive Intent

**Common phrases:**
- "they said they WILL BOOK a call"
- "WILL BOOK"

**Note:** This isn't really an objection - it's a positive signal. These contacts expressed intent to book.

**Insight:** 785 contacts said they would book but may not have followed through. Automated follow-up could convert these.

---

### 4. Doctor/Medical Frustration (9.4%)

**Common phrases:**
- "Dismissed by doctors"
- "Labs were normal but still feel unwell"
- "Doctor refused proper labs"
- "Frustrated by 'normal' labs"
- "Feeling unseen by doctors and system"

**Example quotes:**
- "Doctor said labs were normal but still feel unwell"
- "Dismissive doctors; never told about postnatal depletion"
- "Doctor refused proper labs, wants to know how to start caring for herself again, fear and trauma from prior OB experience"
- "Frustrated that previous labs didn't explain symptoms"

**Insight:** This is a STRONG emotional driver. Messaging should emphasize validation and comprehensive testing.

---

### 5. Program Details/Information Requests (8.8%)

**Common phrases:**
- "What type of tests/labs are included"
- "How does it work"
- "More information"
- "Asked about process"

**Example quotes:**
- "Asked about labs-only option, asked about vegan diets, wants to know if labs distinguish depletion vs. perimenopause"
- "Asked about what type of tests/labs are included"
- "Asked about practitioner credentials"

**Insight:** Better upfront information could reduce these questions. Consider FAQ page or detailed program overview.

---

### 6. Skepticism/Not Ready (11.8%)

**Common phrases:**
- "Concerned"
- "Worried"
- "Not ready to move forward"
- "Wants to talk to husband"

**Example quotes:**
- "Concerned about price, not ready to move forward"
- "Wants to talk to husband"
- "Not ready to make any financial commitments"

**Insight:** This is natural sales friction. Nurture sequences and social proof could help.

---

### 7. Time Constraints (2.1%)

**Common phrases:**
- "Busy"
- "Timing"
- "Finding time with three kids"

**Example quote:**
- "Worried about finding time with three kids at home"

**Insight:** Smallest category. Flexible scheduling and asynchronous options could help.
